📚 How Does Top & Tail Work?— Click to learn
▼

🏭 The Problem

A refinery column or stack stands vertically — 20 to 60 metres tall. To demolish it safely, you need to lay it horizontal first. You can't just grab the top and pull; the base would kick out violently and become a hazard.

⬆↕ The Two-Crane Solution

Main Crane (Top): Connects near the top of the structure using a rigging attachment point. This crane does the heavy lifting — it carries most of the weight and controls the rotation.

Tail Crane (Bottom): Connects near the base. Its job is to CONTROL the bottom end, preventing it from swinging dangerously. It carries a smaller share of the load.

🔑 The Surprising Key Fact

The load on each crane does NOT change as the structure rotates from vertical to horizontal. It stays constant throughout the whole operation. This is because moment (torque) equations cancel out the angle.

T_main = Weight × (CoG height − Tail attach height)
─────────────────────────────────────────────
(Top attach height − Tail attach height)

T_tail = Total Weight − T_main

⚠️ What DOES Change — Crane Radius

As the structure rotates from vertical to horizontal, the top crane's radius increases — the attachment point swings away from the crane. Since crane capacity decreases as radius increases, you must check the crane has enough capacity at its maximum radius (when structure is fully horizontal).

Max Radius = Initial distance + (Top attach − Tail attach) × sin(90°)
= d_crane + (h_top − h_tail)

🎯 Why Two Cranes, Not One?

One crane cannot be in two places at once. For a 30m column, the main crane needs to be positioned to reach the top (25m height) while another crane handles the base. The same GR-800EX can serve as either the main or tail crane depending on its position and the loads.
