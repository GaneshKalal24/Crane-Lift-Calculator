🔗 Top Connection Methods
▼
A — Fabricated Lifting LugRECOMMENDED
Welded to top head or nozzle. Designed by structural engineer, rated 2× WL. WPS-certified welder + NDT weld inspection required. Most common and safest method for refinery columns and stacks.
B — Beam Through ManwaySUITABLE
For large diameter vessels (>1.5m OD). Structural steel beam inserted through top manway. Engineer must confirm vessel shell can carry the point loads at the manway flanges without deformation.
C — Plate Clamp on FlangeSUITABLE
For columns with a heavy top cap plate. Certified plate clamps rated above factored load. Engineer to verify plate thickness, weld quality, and absence of significant corrosion.
D — Choker Sling Around BodyLAST RESORT
Most thin-wall vessels CANNOT withstand the radial crushing force from a sling. Do NOT use on any pressure vessel without a shell collapse calculation signed by engineer. Add spreader beam. Avoid if possible.
📖 How This Operation Works
▼

Why two cranes?

A 30m column has attachment points 25m apart. One crane physically cannot be in two places at once. The GR-800EX handles the top (heavy end), the GR-400EX controls the bottom (lighter — usually 20–40% of total weight).

The pre-tension trick — why it matters

The crane takes up the full weight before the cut. When the oxy-cut is complete, there is no "shock" — the crane is already loaded. This is also your final check that the top connection is sound. If the structure moves unexpectedly: STOP.

The pendulum danger (Phase 3)

Your 32m column hanging from a boom tip is a 32m pendulum. If the crane slews too fast, the bottom swings outward. At 2 m/s slew, a 30m pendulum can swing 3–4m outward. This dramatically increases the effective radius and can cause overload. Slew speed < 1 m/s. Use taglines.

The constant load fact (Phase 5)

As the structure rotates 0°→90°, the load on each crane stays completely constant. This surprises most people. The angle cancels out. What changes is only the main crane's radius — which increases, reducing chart capacity. Always check at 90°.

T_main = W × (h_CoG − h_tail) ÷ (h_top − h_tail)
T_tail = W − T_main (both constant throughout rotation)

Wind limits

AS 1418 and most site procedures limit crane operations to Beaufort 5 (38 km/h) maximum. For tall suspended structures, Beaufort 4 (28 km/h) is a more conservative and safer limit. Stop all operations and secure the structure in any sudden gust above these limits.
