🔗 Top Connection Methods
▼
A — Fabricated Lifting LugRECOMMENDED
Welded to top head or nozzle by WPS-certified welder. Designed by structural engineer, rated 2× working load. NDT weld inspection required before use.
B — Beam Through ManwaySUITABLE
For vessels >1.5m OD. Structural beam through top manway. Engineer must confirm manway flanges carry point loads without deformation.
C — Plate Clamp on CapSUITABLE
Certified plate clamps rated above factored load. Engineer verifies plate thickness, weld integrity, and corrosion status.
D — Choker Sling Around ShellLAST RESORT
Most thin-wall vessels cannot withstand radial crushing force. Do NOT use on any pressure vessel without shell collapse calculation by engineer.
📐 Engineering — Why It Works This Way
▼

Phase 2: The Most Dangerous Moment

At the instant of oxy-cut separation, the crane goes from zero to full vessel weight instantaneously. PRO-028 §7.1.3 mandates ×1.5 factor precisely for this dynamic shock. The ×1.5 factor covers the impact load — without pre-tension the crane would see 2–3× the static weight at separation.

Phase 3: The Pendulum Problem

A 32m column hanging from a boom tip is a 32m pendulum with a period of ~11 seconds. At 1 m/s slew speed, centripetal acceleration adds approximately 0.1g radially, swinging the base outward by 2–4m. This increases effective radius, reducing chart capacity. Taglines are mandatory — they are not optional.

Swing angle ≈ arctan(v² / (g·L))
v = slew tip speed (m/s), L = pendulum length (m)
Extra radius = L × sin(swing angle)

Phase 5: Constant Load Physics

As the vessel rotates from vertical to horizontal, the load on each crane stays completely constant. The geometry cancels out. What changes is only Crane A's radius — which increases as the boom luffs out, reducing chart capacity. Always check at 90°.

T_main = W × (h_CoG − h_tail) ÷ (h_top − h_tail)
T_tail = W − T_main [both constant 0°→90°]
R_main_at_90° = R_lay + (h_top − h_tail)

GBP Calculation

Ground bearing pressure accounts for dynamic load factor (×1.35 per PRO-028), crane self-weight, and hook load. Each outrigger pad carries one quarter of the total. Compare against geotechnical report bearing capacity — not just visual assessment.

GBP = (GVW + Hook Load) × 1.35 × 9.81 / Pad Area (kPa)
