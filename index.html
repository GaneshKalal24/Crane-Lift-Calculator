🔵 Phase 4 — The 40t Crane Connects at the BOTTOM

The tail crane (40t) attaches near the BASE of the vessel, not the top. As the main crane lifts the top, the base rises off the ground and swings outward. The 40t tail crane must track backward to follow the rising bottom end, increasing its operating radius throughout the operation.

At θ from vertical:
Tail attach position moves outward by: h_tail × sin(θ)
Tail crane radius = d_standoff + h_tail × sin(θ)

At horizontal (θ=90°): radius = d_standoff + h_tail

✓ What STAYS Constant

The load on each crane does NOT change as the vessel rotates. This is a fundamental result of moment equilibrium — the load share is fixed by the CoG position relative to the attachment points.

T_main = W × (h_cog − h_tail) / (h_top − h_tail)
T_tail = W − T_main

⚠️ What DOES Change — Main Crane Radius

The main crane's operating radius increases from initial stand-off to maximum when the vessel is horizontal. Check crane chart capacity at the maximum radius.

Max main radius = d_main + (h_top − h_tail)
Check: chartCap(max_radius) > hookLoad_main × DynFactor
