📚 How Pick & Slew Laydown Works— Click to learn
▼

🏭 Phase 1: The Vertical Pick

A refinery column stands vertically on its footing. The Main Crane connects to the top trunnions. The base is cut. During this vertical phase and subsequent slew, the Main Crane holds 100% of the weight, plus the violent dynamic shock factor of the demolition drop.

⬆↕ Phase 2: Tandem Down-Ending

Once transited to the laydown area, the Tail Crane attaches to the bottom trunnions. As the operators tilt the column horizontally, the load shifts. The Main Crane's load decreases, while the Tail Crane takes up its portion of the weight.

⚠️ The Radius Hazard

Even though the Main Crane's load decreases as the column goes horizontal, its working radius increases because the top attachment point swings outward. You must ensure the crane has sufficient chart capacity at this final, maximum radius.

Max Radius = Initial distance + (Top attach − Tail attach)
